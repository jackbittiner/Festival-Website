Leila Herandi

She is a writer, performer and theatre-maker based in Manchester. After studying European Theatre Arts at Rose Bruford, she now creates her own work, often by wrapping intimate personal experiences in her light-hearted, tongue-in-cheek style. At Bruford she was performing in 'The Panopticon Illusion', ‘Pornography’ by Simon Stephens, and ‘Today Is My 100th Birthday’, which was a devised show based on Tadeusz Kantor.

Ina Colizza

She is a choreographer trained in ballet and contemporary dance. She has danced in various projects including “Welcome on board”, choreographed by Francesco Nappa; “Stranded” with “surFace Dance” in London at the First Sound and Movement Symposium; “Here not here, where?” a project at the Royal College of Music of London as part of the Network Performing Arts Production Workshop 2015. With CoDa Dance at Sadler’s Wells, she has performed at Lilian Baylis Theater as part of their Wild Card programme sith a piece called “12 months”. With Shelly Owen she performed in 2 outdoors projects: “Resolution” and “Speed dating”. She followed Akram Khan choreographer as part of the “Creative Fellows” program for the MIF – Manchester International Festival launched for the show Giselle with the English National Ballet.

Plume

Plume are a four piece group based in Manchester that have a distinctive sound influenced by electronic music. The band fuse driving grooves and lush vocals to create an immersive soundscape. Taking influence from the electronic and bass music scene, their quality of musicianship and attention to sound is clear. Their live sets are often through composed, and often involve collaborations with visual artists.

B&B

"Me and B, B and B, we are very glad that you reading this text about us. We are truly crazy happy about it! We are two blond girls, like twins, but actually we are not. We both like to dance, sing, travel and spread love all over the world. Feel free to meet B and B!" B&B is an on-going project. B&B characters were born in the summertime of 2015 in Blank canvas residency Cork town, Ireland. Two Lithuanian choreographers and performers - Greta Grineviciute and Agniete Lisickinaite created these two characters as politically active artistic figures who reflect on environment expressing their feelings, opinions and social encounters. Moreover, they are active media figures constantly questioning the need and purpose of fame and popularity. Stage performance "B and B Dialogue" were nominated for Lithuanian Golden Cross award 2017.

Joe Beedles

Upon premiering his work at The Banff Centre in March 2016, Joe has continued to develop his live set both technically and conceptually, culminating in solo performances, particularly within the Algorave movement and as part of Splice Festival in London. Releasing under the moniker Native, his EP 'Polaris' was released in 2016 on London based label Laura Lies In. His output is varied with stylistic themes of vocal manipulation, shimmering harmonic structures, swathes of textural ambience and deconstructed technoise. Visually, simple geometry and 3D models are abstracted real-time in a direct audio-reactive fashion creating a pseudo-synesthetic experience.

Joe Cook

Joe Cook a.k.a Cookie is a spoken word artist from Birmingham UK. He delivers social commentary in lyrical rhythmic style. Most recently Joe has been performing heavily across Birmingham and The Black Country from headlining poetry nights, performing at WHY? Festival, an event championing disability arts, to performing alongside bands. Joe has also been running lyric writing workshops with various different education and art organizations such as City of Birmingham Schools, Birmingham Conservatoire, Birmingham School of Acting and Birmingham Townhall & Symphony Hall.
